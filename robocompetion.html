Robotics Competitions

Challenge yourself and showcase your robotics skills in our exciting competitions

Our Competitions

Robo Football

Teams develop autonomous or remote-controlled robots to compete in a modified soccer match, requiring strategic programming and precise control systems.

Difficulty: Intermediate to Advanced Team Size: 3-5 members

Competition Details:

  • Robot size limit: 30cm x 30cm x 30cm
  • Match duration: 2 rounds of 5 minutes each
  • Teams must design defensive and offensive strategies
  • Robots must detect and manipulate a specialized ball

Line Follower

Participants build robots that autonomously follow a black line on a white surface, navigating through various challenges like curves, intersections, and breaks.

Difficulty: Intermediate to Advanced Team Size: 1-2 members

Competition Details:

  • Course includes sharp turns, gaps, and intersections
  • Scoring based on completion time and accuracy
  • Robots must use sensors to detect the line
  • Maximum dimensions: 20cm x 20cm x 20cm

Robo Race

A high-speed obstacle course for remote-controlled robots where participants navigate through challenging terrain while racing against the clock.

Difficulty: Intermediate to Advanced Team Size: 1-2 members

Competition Details:

  • Obstacle course with ramps, tunnels, and barriers
  • Time penalties for hitting obstacles
  • Multiple rounds with increasing difficulty
  • Focus on speed, control, and maneuverability

Circuit Debugging

Participants are presented with faulty electronic circuits and must identify and fix issues using diagnostic tools and their knowledge of electronics.

Difficulty: Beginner to Intermediate Team Size: Individual

Competition Details:

  • Timed rounds with increasing complexity
  • Requires troubleshooting skills and electronics knowledge
  • Participants must document their diagnostic process
  • Tools and test equipment provided

CAD Modeling

A design competition where participants create detailed 3D models of robotic systems or components using professional CAD software within a time limit.

Difficulty: Beginner to Intermediate Team Size: Individual

Competition Details:

  • Design based on provided specifications
  • Judged on accuracy, efficiency, and creativity
  • Must include assembly instructions
  • Participants must bring their own laptops with CAD software

Robo Sumo

Robots compete in a sumo-style contest where the objective is to push the opponent out of a circular ring without being pushed out first.

Difficulty: Intermediate to Advanced Team Size: 1-2 members

Competition Details:

  • Weight classes: Lightweight (500g) and Heavyweight (3kg)
  • Round-robin tournament format with elimination finals
  • Robots must include autonomous detection systems
  • Matches last 3 minutes or until one robot is pushed out

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I register for competitions?

Registration for all competitions is available through our online portal. Robo Club members can register with their membership ID for discounted fees. Non-members can also participate by filling out the general registration form and paying the standard entry fee. Registration typically opens 4 weeks before the competition and closes 1 week prior to the event date. Team registrations require information for all team members.

What are the eligibility requirements?

Most competitions are open to undergraduate and graduate students. Some events have specific divisions for different experience levels (beginner, intermediate, advanced). High school students may participate in certain competitions with parental consent. For team-based competitions, all team members must be enrolled students, though they can be from different institutions. Some advanced competitions may require participants to have previous experience or to have qualified through preliminary rounds.

Do I need to bring my own equipment?

For most competitions, participants are required to bring their own robots and equipment. The Robo Club provides the competition arena, power outlets, and basic tools. For competitions like Circuit Debugging, we provide the circuit boards and components. CAD Modeling requires participants to bring their own laptops with installed software. We recommend checking the specific equipment requirements for each competition, which are detailed on the competition registration page.

Are there prizes for winners?

Yes, all competitions offer prizes for top performers. Typically, first, second, and third place winners receive trophies and certificates. Many competitions also include cash prizes or sponsored awards from industry partners. The prize pool varies by competition, with major events offering larger rewards. Additionally, exceptional participants may receive special recognition or invitations to represent the university at national-level competitions. All participants receive a certificate of participation, and special awards are given for innovation and technical excellence.

Can I participate in multiple competitions?

Yes, you can register for multiple competitions during the same event, provided there are no scheduling conflicts. We design the competition schedule to minimize overlaps between events. If you're participating in multiple competitions, you'll receive a discounted registration fee for additional events. However, we recommend carefully considering your capacity to prepare for multiple events, as each competition requires significant preparation and focus. For team-based competitions, team composition may vary between different events.

How can I prepare for competitions?

The Robo Club offers several resources to help participants prepare. We host weekly workshop sessions where you can learn relevant skills and techniques. For each competition, detailed rules and guidelines are available on our website. We also organize pre-competition practice sessions where you can test your robots in conditions similar to the actual competition. Additionally, mentors and previous winners are available for consultation during designated office hours. We recommend starting your preparation at least 4-6 weeks before the competition date.

What are the judging criteria?

Judging criteria vary by competition type. For performance-based competitions like Line Follower and Robo Race, results are based on objective measures such as completion time and accuracy. For design-oriented competitions like CAD Modeling, judging includes creativity, technical complexity, practicality, and presentation quality. All judging panels include faculty members and industry professionals. The complete judging criteria for each competition are provided in the competition handbook, which is shared with all registered participants two weeks before the event.
